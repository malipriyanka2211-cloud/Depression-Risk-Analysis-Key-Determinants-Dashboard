# Depression Risk Analysis – Power BI Dashboard

##  Overview
This project analyzes mental health factors using a Kaggle dataset, focusing on depression risk and its key determinants.  
The Power BI dashboard provides interactive insights into how academic/work pressure, sleep duration, gender, financial stress, and lifestyle factors influence depression patterns.

##  Dataset
- Source: Kaggle – Human Mental Health Factors (cross‑sectional data collected at a single point in time)
- Records: 4,289 individuals
- Key attributes: Academic pressure, work/study hours, sleep duration, dietary habits, suicidal thoughts, financial stress, job satisfaction, profession, city.

##  Dashboard Features
- **Academic Pressure by Degree** – Compare stress levels across education streams.
- **Depression by Gender** – Visualize distribution between male and female.
- **Depression by Sleep Duration** – Identify risk patterns linked to sleep habits.
- **Financial Stress by Gender** – Assess stress levels across genders.
- **Depression vs. Job Satisfaction by City** – Explore city‑wise correlations.
- **Filters** – Profession, dietary habits, suicidal thoughts.

##  Tools & Skills
- **Power BI** – Data modeling, DAX, interactive dashboards
- **SQL/Python** – Data cleaning & preprocessing
- **Statistics** – Risk factor analysis

##  How to Use
1. Clone/download this repository.
2. Open the `.pbix` file in Power BI Desktop.
3. Interact with filters and visuals to explore insights.

##  Insights
- Academic and work pressure strongly correlate with depression risk.
- Sleep duration plays a critical role in mental health outcomes.
- Financial stress levels are consistent across genders.
- City‑level analysis reveals varying patterns of depression vs. job satisfaction.
